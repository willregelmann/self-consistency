## Summary of the position

The 2026-06-10 demotion (PR #72) did not merely weaken one theorem in one paper. It knocked out the result that the repository's own documents designate as "the rigorous Level 2 anchor" of the entire self-consistency hierarchy, and it did so in a way that the autonomous system, **as currently configured, is structurally incapable of repairing**: no OBJECTIVES milestone covers the repair, the scout may only open issues for OBJECTIVES milestones (AUTONOMY.md, "Objective functions"), and PR #72's suggested follow-up issues were never opened. Worse, two of the three existing FPE milestones (FPE-2, FPE-3) have done-conditions that are unreachable or incoherent while the demotion gaps stand, and the one agent-ready FPE issue (#66) contains a context block that still asserts the demoted result as Rigorous. Unless the governor adds explicit repair milestones and reorders the program around them, the experiment will spend its 90 days building extensions on a foundation it has formally acknowledged is broken.

## The argument

### 1. The repair is structurally unreachable without an OBJECTIVES edit — so this is precisely a governor decision, not something the loop will self-correct

Under AUTONOMY.md, "The scout opens issues only for OBJECTIVES milestones," and only the governor edits OBJECTIVES. There is no milestone covering M1/M3–M5, M6/M7, M2, or M8/M9; therefore no scout run, ever, will open a repair issue; therefore no worker will ever claim one. The only mechanism by which the experiment's most important known defect gets fixed is the edit this debate decides on. Declining to add repair milestones is not deferring the repair — it is *deciding against it* for the remainder of the experiment. (Process claim; grounded in AUTONOMY.md "Objective functions" and the OBJECTIVES/issue record above.)

### 2. The existing milestones are corrupted by the demotion — they cannot simply be left in place at current priority

- **FPE-3 is explicitly flagged as broken.** PR #72's own text: "Note FPE-3 in OBJECTIVES.md presupposes the now-demoted fixed point and should be re-scoped by the governor." A stability analysis around a fixed point whose existence proof has four named gaps is stability of an object not known (at the claimed level) to exist. Working FPE-3 before FPE-4 inverts the logical dependency. (Process claim; grounded in PR #72 body.)
- **FPE-2's done-condition is unreachable while M6/M7 stand.** FPE-2 promises that establishing A3 would "promot[e] Schauder existence from Conditional." But the written Schauder proof has two acknowledged defects independent of A3: K_ρ is not closed/convex as defined (M6) and the radius selection is incorrect (M7) (`index.tex` lines 508, 518). Establish A3 tomorrow and the promotion still cannot happen. The Schauder repair (proposed FPE-5) **blocks** FPE-2 in the METHODOLOGY.md relation sense. (Process claim grounded in the gap notes; that M6/M7 are repairable as flagged is the demotion PR's own characterization — **(Conjecture)** until someone actually repairs them.)
- **FPE-1's issue (#66) is built on the withdrawn record.** Issue #66's context block states "Lemma 3 of the paper establishes (Rigorous): ‖K^red‖ ≤ C_K·ħm²/(4π)²" and cites the 2026-03-02 exploration — whose verdict was withdrawn 2026-06 (README, Explorations section). That ħm² scaling is gap **M4** (asserted, not derived); the exponential-decay step it leans on is gap **M3** (elliptic substituted for hyperbolic). A worker claiming #66 today would attempt to extend to m = 0 a bound that is no longer established for m > 0, checking limiting cases against a formula the paper now flags as possibly wrong on both sides. The massless case is genuinely interesting, but its *foundation* is the thing FPE-4 would repair. At minimum #66 needs a context refresh before it is safe to work; at best it should be sequenced after FPE-4. (Process claim; grounded in issue #66 body vs. `index.tex` gap notes and README line 38.)

So the choice is not "repair milestones vs. the existing healthy roadmap." All three existing milestones are degraded by the demotion: one explicitly needs re-scoping (FPE-3), one has an unreachable done-condition (FPE-2), and one has a stale, hazard-bearing issue (FPE-1). The roadmap must be rebuilt around the repair regardless; the only question is whether to admit it.

### 3. Leverage: one repair re-validates claims in two other programs; any other available work advances exactly one

The co-emergence signature-blindness result B1 — a headline structural claim of that program — is now Sketch *solely because* "the underlying Banach contraction [was] demoted 2026-06" (co-emergence README, key-results table). Re-promoting the contraction re-promotes B1's foundation in the same stroke; PR #84's site list is effectively a pre-computed map of everything FPE-4 would heal. No item in the current agent-ready queue has cross-program fan-out remotely comparable: #91 is cosmetic, #33 is a numerics diagnostic, #29 advances one CE claim. Honesty requires noting the one counterweight: GGD's README states its decoherence prediction "does not depend on those existence results," so the fan-out is two programs' *claims-structure* (FPE + CE), not three. (Process claims; grounded in the cited READMEs and PR #84.)

### 4. The experiment's credibility metric favors demonstrated repair over greenfield extension

The demotion was experimenter-authored, two days into the autonomous window. The system's first substantive answer to it is a direct test of the AUTONOMY.md thesis that "demotion is normal maintenance": a healthy autonomous research program responds to a demotion by closing the gaps or documenting the obstruction — not by routing all worker capacity to adjacent greenfield work while the program's own README opens with a withdrawal notice. A 90-day record that reads "anchor demoted on day 1, repair never scheduled" is a worse experimental outcome than "anchor demoted on day 1, repair attempted, obstruction documented" *even if the repair fails*, because the obstruction-documentation outcome is itself admissible under every proposed done-condition below. (Process claim about the experiment design; grounded in METHODOLOGY.md Rigor Lifecycle and EXPERIMENT.md's framing via AUTONOMY.md.)

### 5. The repair is plausibly tractable — and where it is not, the milestone is still satisfiable

- M6/M7 are characterized in the demotion PR itself as "repairable" proof-engineering defects (set definition, radius selection) — standard fixed-point-theorem bookkeeping. **(Conjecture** that the repair succeeds; the characterization is the record's.)
- M5 (self-mapping bound) is a standard, well-understood component of any contraction argument; its absence is an omission, not a known obstruction. **(Conjecture)**
- M1/M3/M4 are harder: M1 may resolve to either sign of the κ scaling, and M3 requires genuinely Lorentzian kernel control. The literature anchor exists — Meda–Pinamonti–Siemssen's FLRW contraction for massive fields (already cited in-paper and in issue #66's consistency checks) shows the *type* of theorem is provable. **(Sketch** that a Lorentzian route exists in symmetric cases, by citation to MPS; **Conjecture** for the general compact-Cauchy-surface case.)
- Crucially, every proposed milestone below carries an "or document the obstruction" branch — matching FPE-1's existing pattern — so milestone completion does not gamble on the mathematics cooperating. A documented obstruction to the Banach route would itself be high-value: it would tell co-emergence to re-anchor Level 2 on Starobinsky + repaired Schauder rather than waiting.

## Where this position is underspecified

1. **No independent mathematical assessment of repairability.** I have not attempted M1/M3/M4/M5 myself. The claim that FPE-4 is tractable rests on (a) the MPS precedent for symmetric spacetimes and (b) the demotion PR's own framing — both could be misleading. If M3 (genuine Lorentzian kernel control on a general compact-Cauchy-surface background) is as hard as the open literature suggests it might be, FPE-4 could consume many worker-days and end in the obstruction branch. The position survives that outcome only because the done-condition admits it — but the *priority* argument weakens if the expected outcome is obstruction-documentation rather than re-promotion.
2. **M1's resolution may destroy, not repair, the headline claim.** If the dimensionally consistent reading is κ ∝ 1/R, the κ ~ (m/M_P)² « 1 statement and the small-coupling narrative around it do not survive in their current form, and the right disposition might be retirement of the Banach route plus re-anchoring co-emergence on Starobinsky + Schauder. My proposed FPE-4 done-condition technically covers this ("retired status"), but the position's rhetoric of "repair" presumes the optimistic branch.
3. **M8/M9 are excluded from the proposal.** PR #72 flags function-space conflation (M8) and state-selection well-definedness (M9) as open structural problems that *predate* the demotion. If M8/M9 are fatal, repairing M1/M3–M5 polishes a proof of an ill-posed statement. I left them out to keep milestones bounded; a fair synthesis should ask whether FPE-4 must include a well-posedness sub-condition.
4. **Worker-capacity arithmetic is hand-waved.** I have not estimated whether FPE-4+5+6 fit the experiment's remaining 88 days alongside the existing queue, and issue #81 (experimenter-priority, co-emergence) outranks everything in the worker's own ranking regardless of OBJECTIVES — so the near-term effect of this edit operates through the scout, with latency I have not modeled.
5. **The GGD fan-out is weak.** GGD explicitly states its prediction does not depend on the FPE existence results; my "two other programs" framing is honest in the claims-structure sense but the *physics* fan-out is essentially co-emergence alone. An opposing position could legitimately argue the anchor language overstates FPE's load-bearing role and that the cheapest fix is to weaken the anchor rhetoric rather than repair the theorem.
6. **Demotion-as-maintenance cuts both ways.** METHODOLOGY.md insists demotion is normal, not an emergency. A rival position could argue that immediately re-prioritizing everything around a demotion *contradicts* that ethos and teaches the system to panic-repair rather than route around sunk costs. My response — that unreachability of the repair under current OBJECTIVES makes this a now-or-never governance decision — is a process argument, not a refutation.
