## 1. MICRODIAMOND EXPERIMENTS (BMV - Bose-Marletto-Vedral)

### Concept
Two diamond spheres with embedded nitrogen-vacancy (NV) centers are prepared in spin superpositions that translate to spatial superpositions via spin-dependent magnetic forces. They interact gravitationally during free fall, accumulating quantum entanglement. Detection of this entanglement would prove gravity is quantum mechanical.

### Key Numbers
- **Two particles:** m₁ = m₂ = 10⁻¹⁴ kg each
- **Composition:** ~100 billion carbon atoms per diamond
- **Size:** ~90 nm radius (sphere)
- **Separation:** 250 micrometers (crucial to exclude Casimir force)
- **Interaction time:** 3 seconds
- **Spin coherence:** 2-3 milliseconds (room temperature), 0.6 seconds (cryogenic)
- **Spin control:** Microwave pulses create superposition

### Current Status
- **Stage:** Detailed proposal with experimental feasibility studies
- **Leaders:** Sougato Bose (London), Gavin Morley (Sussex)
- **Next step:** Construct single diamond in spatial superposition (proof of concept)
- **Challenge:** Maintaining 250 μm separation against magnetic/vibrational noise
- **Timeline:** Prototype results expected 2026-2027

### Why This Matters
If successful, BMV would be the **first table-top test of quantum gravity** using massive macroscopic objects (~10¹¹ atoms). Null result would constrain quantum gravity models.

## 2. LEVITATED NANOPARTICLE EXPERIMENTS (Optomechanical)

### Concept
Silica nanoparticles are trapped and cooled using optical tweezers in ultra-high vacuum and cryogenic temperatures. Position control reaches quantum ground state. Two particles could be entangled via gravitational coupling, or single particles used to test quantum superposition limits.

### Key Numbers (Femtogram Scale - Most Advanced)
- **Particle mass:** 10⁻¹⁸ kg (5 femtograms)
- **Material:** Silica (SiO₂)
- **Size:** ~100-200 nm radius
- **Separation:** 500 nm (projected for gravity tests)
- **Temperature:** 5-10 K environment, 5 mK particle
- **Coherence time:** 1-100 seconds (demonstrated)
- **Zero-point motion:** ~5 picometers (fundamental limit)

### Current Status
- **Stage:** Quantum control demonstrated (2021-2024)
- **Recent achievements:**
  - Ground-state cooling of single mechanical mode (Science, 2020)
  - Two-mode ground-state cooling (Nature Physics, 2023)
  - Multi-particle arrays (25+ spheres, 2024)
  - Cavity-mediated interactions between particles
- **Leaders:** Markus Aspelmeyer (Vienna), Oriol Romero-Isart (Barcelona), others
- **Challenge:** Expanding spatial superpositions beyond current ~nm limits while maintaining coherence
- **Timeline:** Gravity test feasibility 2027-2029

### Why This Matters
Optomechanical systems **bridge quantum and classical regimes** with macroscopic masses while retaining quantum control. Ideal for testing gravity-induced decoherence predictions.

## 3. MAQRO MISSION (Macroscopic Quantum Resonators)

### Concept
Space-based satellite carrying optical levitation experiments. Ultra-low vacuum (10⁻¹⁰ mbar) and microgravity (10⁻⁹ g) eliminate terrestrial decoherence sources. Long coherence times (100+ seconds) enable unambiguous tests of quantum gravity.

### MAQRO-PF (PathFinder) - 2025 Proposal

**Two Design Options:**
1. **Small:** 10-20 U CubeSat, 5-10 kg, possible ISS attachment
2. **Large:** 1 m³ satellite, 150 kg, dedicated LEO mission

### Key Numbers
- **Test particle mass:** 10¹⁰ amu = 1.66 × 10⁻¹⁷ kg
- **Material:** Silica spheres
- **Size:** ~200-400 nm radius
- **Grating separation:** 100 nm (Talbot interferometry)
- **Interference time:** 100 seconds (Talbot time)
- **Temperature:** ~20 K
- **Vacuum:** 10⁻⁹ g drag-free environment over ~200 second evolution
- **Platform requirement:** Temperature stability, vibration isolation on spacecraft

### Current Status
- **Stage:** Mission concept, MAQRO-PF white paper (December 2025)
- **Next phase:** Technology demonstration, pathfinder mission prep
- **Timeline:** PathFinder launch 2028-2032; full MAQRO 2033-2037
- **Funding:** ESA Advanced Research Concepts, national space agencies

### Why This Matters
MAQRO would provide **definitive test of Diósi-Penrose and other quantum gravity models** with macroscopic masses in genuine quantum superposition. First space-based quantum gravity experiment.

## 4. DIOSI-PENROSE MODEL - THE PREDICTION BEING TESTED

### Theory Overview
Gravity is not truly quantum; wave function collapse occurs because gravitational self-energy cannot support quantum superpositions indefinitely.

**One free parameter:** Gravitational localization radius R₀ ~ 10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁸ meters

### Collapse Rate Formula (Simplified)
```
1/τ_collapse ~ (R₀/m) × (ΔE_gravitational / ℏ)
```

where ΔE_gravitational is the energy difference between mass distributions in different branches.

### Testable Predictions
1. **Larger masses decohere faster** - macroscopic objects shouldn't be in superposition
2. **Larger spatial separations decohere faster** - superposition branches far apart collapse quickly
3. **Gravitational entanglement is possible** - if two masses interact via gravity, their wave functions entangle before collapsing
4. **Survival time for macroscopic superpositions > 1 day** - for reasonable lab parameters

### Test Configurations
- BMV 250 μm separation: τ ~ 10-100 s (predicted)
- Levitated 500 nm separation: τ ~ 1-100 s (predicted)
- MAQRO 100 nm separation: τ ~ 100+ s (predicted)

## 11. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Why is 250 micrometers chosen for microdiamond separation?**
A: It's large enough to exclude the Casimir force (~100 nm) while small enough to allow significant gravitational coupling in finite time.

**Q: Why femtogram particles for levitated systems?**
A: Large enough for measurable gravity; small enough for quantum cooling and optical control. Sweet spot around 10⁻¹⁸ kg.

**Q: Why would MAQRO need 100 seconds coherence?**
A: Gravitational decoherence is very slow (~10⁻⁴¹ rate for typical masses). Long times needed to accumulate measurable signal above noise.

**Q: If nothing gravitational shows up, does that kill quantum gravity theories?**
A: No - it constrains parameter space and favors theories with slower decoherence or different signatures. Null results still provide valuable data.

**Q: Can these experiments run simultaneously?**
A: Potentially yes. Optomechanical systems are table-top; microdiamonds and space missions are independent. Consistency checks across platforms strengthen conclusions.
