## 1. MICRODIAMOND EXPERIMENTS (BMV - Bose-Marletto-Vedral)

### 1.1 Microdiamond Mass and Structure

**Mass:**
- Typical mass: m = 10⁻¹⁴ kg
- Range: 10⁻¹⁵ to 10⁻¹⁴ kg for superposition tests

**Number of Atoms:**
- ~10¹¹ to 10¹² atoms per microdiamond (100-1000 billion atoms)
- Literature reference: "more than 100 billion carbon atoms"

**Diamond Material Properties:**
- Density: ρ = 3.5 g/cm³ (or 3.52 g/cm³)
- Lattice constant: a = 3.567 Å = 0.3567 nm = 3.567 × 10⁻¹⁰ m
- Unit cell: 8 carbon atoms per unit cell
- Atomic density: ~1.76 × 10²⁹ atoms/m³
- Volume of a 10⁻¹⁴ kg diamond: V = m/ρ = (10⁻¹⁴ kg)/(3500 kg/m³) ≈ 2.86 × 10⁻¹⁸ m³ ≈ 2860 nm³

**Derived Sphere Radius:**
- From V = (4/3)πr³: r ≈ 90 nm (approximate radius of microdiamond)

### 1.2 Superposition Parameters

**Superposition Separation:**
- Center-of-mass separation distance: d = 250 μm
- This is the key parameter to separate gravitational effects from Casimir interactions

**Wavepacket Size (spatial extent σ):**
- Each branch spatial extent: σ = 50-100 nm (estimated from NV center quantum control capabilities)
- Literature suggests: "microwave pulse" control of NV center superposition

**Coherence Times (NV Center T₂):**
- Room temperature Hahn-echo coherence time: T₂ ≈ 1.8-2.4 ms (optimized samples)
- Room temperature typical: T₂ ~ 100-600 μs
- With dynamical decoupling: T₂ ~ 1.5 ms (extended by factor ~200-400)
- At 77 K with dynamical decoupling: T₂ ≈ 0.6 s
- Coherence time limited by paramagnetic impurities and spin-phonon coupling

### 1.3 Experimental Setup - NV Center Control

**Nitrogen-Vacancy Center Properties:**
- Embedded nitrogen atom adjacent to crystal vacancy
- Microwave pulses create superposition of spin states
- Spin states: |↓⟩, |0⟩, |↑⟩ (ground state and excited states)
- Optical readout of spin state via fluorescence

**Creating Center-of-Mass Superposition:**
- Spin-dependent force from magnetic field gradient
- Each spin state experiences different force → spatial separation
- Time to develop full 250 μm separation: ~3 seconds
- Evolution: free fall in Earth's gravity field with spin-dependent deflection

### 1.4 Proposed Coherence Duration and Entanglement Growth

**Timeline for Single Experiment:**
- Duration of mutual interaction: ~3 seconds (time for particles to fall side-by-side)
- Gravitational entanglement accumulates during this time
- Required decoherence rate limit for signature observation: must preserve coherence over 3+ seconds

**Temperature Environment:**
- Room temperature (~300 K) baseline
- Reduced temperature (cryogenic) would extend T₂, enabling longer experiments

### 1.5 Detection Method

**Entanglement Witness Protocol:**
- Measure spin states of nitrogen-vacancy systems in both diamonds
- Bell inequality test to detect gravitational entanglement
- Two diamonds enter separate spin measurement devices

## 2. OPTOMECHANICAL OSCILLATOR EXPERIMENTS (Levitated Nanoparticles)

### 2.1 Nanoparticle Parameters

**Mass Range:**
- Femtogram scale: 10⁻¹⁵ grams = 10⁻¹⁸ kg
- Nanogram scale: 10⁻⁹ grams = 10⁻¹² kg
- Recent experiments: typical range 10⁻¹⁵ to 10⁻¹⁸ kg

**Material:**
- Silica (SiO₂) nanoparticles (dielectric)
- Spherical shape (typically)

**Size/Radius:**
- Femtogram particles: r ~ 50-200 nm
- Nanogram particles: r ~ 500 nm to 1 μm
- Determined by optical trapping requirements

### 2.2 Levitation Parameters

**Optical Trapping:**
- Laser-induced dipole force from focused laser beam
- Wavelength: typically ~1064 nm (Nd:YAG)
- Laser power: tunable, typically 1-10 W

**Vacuum Conditions:**
- Pressure: 10⁻⁵ mbar (typical)
- Drag-free environment: 10⁻⁹ g required for MAQRO-level experiments
- Cooling to ground state requires ultra-high vacuum: 10⁻⁷ mbar or better

### 2.3 Temperature Achievements

**Cryogenic Cooling (Recent 2021-2023):**
- Optically levitated nanoparticles in cryogenic free space
- Environment temperature: ~10 K
- Particle center-of-mass cooling: reach millikelvin regime
- Cavity cooling along optical axis: ~1-10 mK achieved
- Perpendicular modes: ~100-500 mK

**Magnetic Levitation (Alternative):**
- Superconducting microspheres at millikelvin temperatures
- Temperature: ~15 mK
- High quality factor Q > 10⁷

### 2.4 Quantum Ground State Cooling

**Recent Achievements (2021-2023):**
- Ground-state cooling of single motional mode achieved
- Two-dimensional ground-state cooling demonstrated
- Measurement backaction becomes dominant decoherence mechanism
- Zero-point motion ≈ 1-10 pm for femtogram particles

### 2.5 Key Experimental Groups and Platforms

**Markus Aspelmeyer (University of Vienna):**
- Pioneering levitated optomechanics research
- Advances in cryogenic free-space levitation
- Cavity-mediated interactions between nanoparticles

**Oriol Romero-Isart (ICFO, Barcelona):**
- Theoretical proposals for testing gravity with levitated particles
- "Coherent inflation" techniques for large spatial superpositions
- Predicted feasibility of gravitational decoherence tests

**Multi-particle Trapping (2024):**
- Arrays of 25+ microspheres simultaneously levitated
- Programmable cavity-mediated interactions
- Displacement sensitivity: <1 nm/√Hz with camera tracking

### 2.6 Superposition Parameters for Gravity Tests

**Spatial Separation (projected):**
- Current demonstrations: coherence lengths exceeding zero-point motion by 3-10x
- Projected for gravity tests: 100 nm to 1 μm separations
- Limited by thermal diffusion and photon recoil heating

**Coherence Time:**
- Current baseline: milliseconds to seconds in optimal conditions
- Limited by photon recoil heating and environmental vibrations
- Cryogenic environments: T₂ ~ seconds or longer

## 3. MAQRO (Macroscopic Quantum Resonators) MISSION

### 3.1 Mission Architecture

**MAQRO-PF (PathFinder) Concept (2025 White Paper):**
- Dedicated satellite in low Earth orbit (LEO)
- Two proposed form factors:
  - Small: 10-20 U CubeSat, 5-10 kg, possible ISS payload
  - Large: ~1 m³ satellite, ~150 kg total mass

**Payload Design:**
- 200 mm diameter vacuum chamber
- Zerodur 'floating optical breadboard' (FOB), double-sided
- Two identical optical trapping experiments (simultaneous operation)

### 3.2 Test Particle Specifications

**Mass Range:**
- Goal: test particles with mass up to 10¹⁰ amu
- 1 amu = 1.66054 × 10⁻²⁷ kg
- Maximum test mass: 10¹⁰ amu ≈ 1.66 × 10⁻¹⁷ kg

**Material:**
- Silica (SiO₂) particles
- Typical density: ~2.2 g/cm³

**Particle Size:**
- Radius: few hundred nanometers (e.g., 100-500 nm)
- For 10¹⁰ amu: diameter ~ 200-400 nm

### 3.3 Interference Configuration

**Talbot Time Baseline:**
- For 10¹⁰ amu particles with grating period Λ = 100 nm
- Talbot time T = Λ²/(2h) × m
- Baseline: T ≈ 100 s (coherence evolution time)

**Decoherence Parameter (Λ):**
- For particles with mass ~few 10¹⁰ amu and radius ~few hundred nm
- Predicted gravitational decoherence rate: Λ ~ 10¹¹ Hz/m²

### 3.4 Environmental Requirements

**Vacuum:**
- Drag-free environment: 10⁻⁹ g over measurement periods
- Particle and environment temperature: ≲20 K

**Duration:**
- Extended coherence evolution: ≥10 seconds
- Target: 100 seconds for Talbot time baseline

**Noise Control:**
- Vibration isolation
- Temperature stability
- Residual acceleration budget

### 3.5 Science Objectives

1. Test fundamental limits on quantum superposition size and complexity
2. Probe all decoherence sources (environmental, quantum, gravitational)
3. Search for deviations from quantum mechanics at macroscopic scales
4. Test Diósi-Penrose predictions quantitatively

## 4. DIÓSI-PENROSE MODEL PARAMETERS

### 4.1 Model Overview

**Free Parameter:**
- Gravitational localization radius: R₀
- All collapse rates depend on R₀
- Typical values: R₀ ~ 10⁻⁶ - 10⁻⁸ m (few micrometers to sub-micrometer)

### 4.2 Entanglement Generation Parameters (Recent Results)

**Mass Specifications:**
- Test masses: m₁ = m₂ = 10⁻¹⁵ kg (equal masses for symmetric case)

**Wavepacket Extents (Example Configuration):**
- Branch 1: σ₁ = 50 μm
- Branch 2: σ₂ = 75 μm
- Branch 3: σ₃ = 100 μm
- Branch 4: σ₄ = 250 μm

**Spatial Separation:**
- Superposition separation distance along one axis: d = 24 μm
- Other separations: L = 23 μm
- Distance threshold for entanglement generation: depends on R₀

### 4.3 Gravitationally-Induced Decoherence (GID) Time

**Typical Values:**
- For reasonable experimental parameters (macro-scale masses in superposition)
- Gravitational-induced collapse can survive for >1 day
- Collapse time τ_GID scales as: τ_GID ~ ℏ/(ΔE_grav)
  where ΔE_grav is the gravitational self-energy difference between branches

**Example:** For 10⁻¹⁵ kg mass in 100 nm superposition:
- ΔE_grav ~ 10⁻⁴¹ J (rough estimate)
- τ_GID ~ 10⁸ s (days to weeks)

## 6. NOISE KERNEL INPUTS FOR CALCULATIONS

### 6.1 Parameters Needed for Noise Kernel Computations

For calculating gravitational decoherence rates via the noise kernel formalism:

1. **Source Masses:**
   - m₁, m₂ (two test particles or extended mass)
   - ρ(r) (mass density distribution)

2. **Spatial Configuration:**
   - Separation d between centers-of-mass
   - Wavepacket sizes σ₁, σ₂ (spatial extent of each superposition branch)
   - Gaussian vs. box profile assumptions

3. **Graviton Initial State:**
   - Vacuum state (ground state) assumed
   - Finite temperature effects (T ~ 10 K for MAQRO)

4. **Quantum State:**
   - Energy eigenstate of test particle Hamiltonian
   - Temperature/thermal distribution if applicable

5. **Integration Domain:**
   - UV cutoff: Planck scale or effective field theory cutoff
   - IR cutoff: geometry of apparatus

### 6.2 Reference Configuration for Calculation

**Microdiamond BMV Case:**
- m₁ = m₂ = 10⁻¹⁴ kg
- d = 250 × 10⁻⁶ m
- σ₁ = σ₂ = 50-100 nm
- T ~ 300 K (room temperature)
- Evolution time: t ~ 3 seconds

- m₁ = m₂ = 10⁻¹⁷ kg (femtogram)
- d ~ 100 nm to 1 μm (projected)

## 7. CURRENT EXPERIMENTAL GAPS AND CHALLENGES

### 7.1 Microdiamond Challenges

- Maintaining 250 μm separation with magnetic trapping (drift, vibrations)
- Achieving long NV coherence times (~ms) while manipulating center-of-mass
- Molecular scattering at room temperature (major decoherence source)
- Integration of superposition creation and entanglement witness in single apparatus

### 7.2 Optomechanical Challenges

- Expanding spatial superpositions beyond current ~100 nm limits
- Reducing photon recoil heating below gravitational decoherence rate
- Extending coherence times to seconds range needed for gravity signatures
- Creating two-particle entangled states for gravitational coupling

### 7.3 MAQRO Challenges

- Achieving 10⁻⁹ g drag-free environment in LEO continuously
- Maintaining 20 K temperature in operational satellite
- Creating/manipulating 100 nm scale superpositions in flight
- Telemetry of quantum measurement outcomes from space platform
